# Student Policy Campaign (Age 17)

At age **17**, Jia organized a student-led petition campaign that influenced education policy in Ontario.

Key metrics:

* **15,000 signatures collected in two weeks**
* national media coverage
* policy response from government and school boards

The campaign focused on the **grade freeze issue during the Omicron-interrupted semester**, when students argued that disruptions to schooling made grading unfair.

The challenge was that education policy is structured to resist rapid change:

* multiple bureaucratic layers
* split jurisdiction between school boards and provincial government
* little incentive for officials to respond quickly to student complaints

Jia had no institutional leverage:

* no budget
* no organization behind her
* no formal political power

Instead she focused on creating **public pressure through visibility**.

Her strategy involved:

* distributing the petition simultaneously across school boards in Ontario
* rapidly accumulating signatures
* coordinating media outreach so coverage and signatures amplified each other

The story that ultimately reached national media was not only the policy issue itself but the fact that **a 17-year-old student had mobilized thousands of signatures in a short time frame**.

The campaign gained national coverage:

CBC
[https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/ottawa/student-petition-ocdsb-freeze-marking-1.6319301](https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/ottawa/student-petition-ocdsb-freeze-marking-1.6319301)

National Post
[https://nationalpost.com/pmn/news-pmn/canada-news-pmn/ontario-students-seek-grade-freeze-during-omicron-interrupted-semester](https://nationalpost.com/pmn/news-pmn/canada-news-pmn/ontario-students-seek-grade-freeze-during-omicron-interrupted-semester)

The government eventually responded to the issue following the public attention generated by the campaign.
