# Hackathons (Evidence)

Topic: hackathons

Jiahui has participated in multiple hackathons where teams rapidly prototype experimental technologies.

These environments have been a recurring space for exploring unconventional ideas, testing new technical tools, and collaborating with interdisciplinary teams.

One notable project is Duet, created during Cal Hacks. The project explores how brainwave signals can be translated into music using machine learning. The system connects EEG signals from a brain-computer interface to generative music models, allowing emotional or cognitive states to influence musical output.

She also built a project called Nurobuckle during another hackathon, exploring AI systems designed to monitor or assist with real-world safety scenarios and human behavior.

Jiahui has participated in hackathons connected to major AI ecosystems, including events hosted by communities around Y Combinator, OpenAI, and DeepMind.

Her work in this ecosystem has also led to national recognition, including placing Top 3 in Canada at Ocean Tech, a competition focused on technology innovation for ocean sustainability.
