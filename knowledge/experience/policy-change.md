# Policy change — student petition (age 17)

**Use for:** Policy change, student petition, 15k signatures, age 17, Ontario, grade freeze, CBC/National Post coverage.

At 17, I changed a legislative policy in Canada where adult advocacy groups had failed. **15,000 signatures in two weeks.** No budget, no institution, no sponsor.

**What made it hard:** Canadian education policy is designed to resist this kind of pressure. Multiple bureaucratic layers, split jurisdiction, elected officials with every incentive to wait out student complaints. Adult organizations with real resources had been pushing for weeks. Nothing moved.

**What made it harder:** I had no obvious leverage. No vote, no money, no institutional backing. The only path was to manufacture political risk faster than the system could absorb it.

So I mapped the actual mechanism. Politicians respond to political risk. Political risk requires public salience. Public salience requires media. Media requires a story with unstoppable momentum. I engineered the momentum before the story existed: seeded the petition simultaneously across every major school board in Ontario, pitched journalists before the numbers were undeniable so coverage and signatures compounded together.

The story that broke nationally wasn't just the policy. It was that a 17-year-old had built what adult institutions hadn't.

The government acted.
