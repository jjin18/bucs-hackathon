# SECTION

ID: ai_research_health_ai_trust
Topic: AI Research
Category: research

Content

Jiahui has conducted independent research exploring how trust forms between humans and AI systems in consumer healthcare contexts.

The project titled Health AI Trust investigates how patients interpret responses from health AI assistants.

Traditional AI benchmarks typically focus on correctness and factual accuracy. However, real-world healthcare usage often depends on whether users feel comfortable trusting and acting on AI-generated responses.

The research explores signals that influence trust, including transparency, reasoning clarity, expressions of uncertainty, and tone.

To support this research, she built a small evaluation system that allows AI responses to be compared across models using real patient questions.

The evaluation focuses not only on correctness but also on perceived trustworthiness and communication style.

This work highlights the gap between traditional AI evaluation metrics and the human factors that influence trust in healthcare AI systems.

Jiahui has participated in multiple hackathons where teams rapidly prototype experimental technologies.

These environments have been a recurring space for exploring unconventional ideas, testing new technical tools, and collaborating with interdisciplinary teams.

One notable project is Duet, created during Cal Hacks.

The project explores how brainwave signals can be translated into music using machine learning.

The system connects EEG signals from a brain-computer interface to generative music models, allowing emotional or cognitive states to influence musical output.

This creates an interface where users can compose music through mental focus and emotional patterns rather than traditional instruments.

She also built a project called Nurobuckle during another hackathon.

The project explores AI systems designed to monitor or assist with real-world safety scenarios and human behavior.

Jiahui has participated in hackathons connected to major AI ecosystems, including events hosted by communities around Y Combinator, OpenAI, and DeepMind.

These events bring together engineers, founders, and researchers to prototype experimental AI systems.
